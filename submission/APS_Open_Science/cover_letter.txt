To the Editorial Board
APS Open Science

Dear Editors,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "A two-feedback Vicsek-Couzin
flock: dense-phase noise rectification as the unifying action of
density-gated motility-noise coupling," for publication in APS Open
Science as an original research article. The Vicsek model fixes the
self-propulsion speed and the angular-noise distribution as global
constants; a density-dependent speed (which drives motility-induced phase
separation) and heavy-tailed Levy reorientations have each been relaxed
separately. We ask what happens when both are gated together by one
density-dependent sigmoid in the local neighbour count, isolating each
channel with a four-way ablation on a single code path.

Heavy-tailed noise alone grows no size-dependent susceptibility; only the
motility channel builds a phase-separated dense phase, inside which the
noise-shape channel rectifies its Cauchy kicks into Gaussian ones. What at
first looks like a super-additive cooperation is, we show with a
matched-noise control, a single effect: equalising the effective dense
decorrelation collapses the susceptibility excess (Delta_9 from +0.45 to
+0.02) and reverses the static g(r) and dynamic C(tau) gaps, so the two
channels are additive and the noise-shape feedback simply lowers the dense
effective noise. The robust results are a two-regime split that reproduces
across three densities, a density-gated Levy-to-Gaussian conversion of
dense-phase turning, and a decoupled-sigmoid dissection pinning the
motility threshold as the sole, metric-kernel control.

The work suits APS Open Science and its broad, open-access remit:
it is a careful, well-controlled active-matter study whose central result
is clarifying and whose null results (a passive noise-shape threshold,
negligible hysteresis, no extended patch lifetime, a topological-kernel
breakdown, a falsified mean-density prediction) are reported rather than
buried, with the finite-size slopes framed as crossover rates, not
asymptotic exponents. All code and data are openly available at
https://github.com/yayayou47/two-feedback-vicsek for bit-for-bit
reproduction of every figure. The manuscript is original, is not under
consideration elsewhere, and its two authors declare no competing
interests; one supplementary-material PDF accompanies it.

Thank you for considering our submission.
